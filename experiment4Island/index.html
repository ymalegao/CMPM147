Experiment 4 - Alternate Worlds

Description

You are traversing a vast ocean, with different depths, and there are periodically islands spawning around you

Technical

I used noise to know which tiles were going to be island and which tiles would be water. I did not have to change the tile generation code for this. After the water was spawned, I used noise again to add some nice depth affect on the water, since that is how deep water is portrayed in pokemon. On the islands, I spawned palm trees, and then made it so that if you clicked it would make a ripple in the water. I also tried to add some autotiling logic once again, so that the island that interacted with water would have curves on it

Reflection

I think this world looks the best, and the islands spawn according to the world key as well. I think I mostly struggled with spawning and coloring the islands, but after the islands were intially spawned, I thought that I should implement autotiling, and it went really well. I think once the islands and water were set, that was the most diffucult part, after that it was just adding decorations.

Artistic Statemnt

Infinity: This world is vast ocean with islands scattered around. This extends into infinity in all directions. Variation in Space: The water have a deeper and darker blue to indicate depth. The islands have palm trees and autotiling implemented Variation in Key: The world key determines the islands that spawn, and the water depth, it is used to control the noise Signs of Life: Clicking on the water will create a ripple in the water
